Vision of Islands

A non-profit eye surgical and clinical team that visits the Pacific Islands, with emphasis on remote areas and involving the local medical staff and relevant Health Ministry.

About us

Let Us Tell You Our Story

We are a non-profit volunteer eye surgical and clinical team who provide our services free of charge in the Pacific islands, so far extending to Tonga and Tuvalu.

We work under the direction of the Ministry of Health of each participating country to provide medical outreach team visits, involving and training the local medical staff in patient care, providing medical and teaching equipment and train local staff in their use, and extending our specialist eye services to remote areas where people lack specialist services because of remoteness. We bring with us all required equipment, expendables, teaching materials (with backup) and on leaving to take away all unwanted items, rubbish so that we leave the island as clean or cleaner than on arrival.

Our goal is to include Registrar trainees from Australasia in the program to expose them to aspects of humanitarian care where they will also see extreme need and advanced pathology. We also aim to take other professionals who may wish to serve or study topics covering several island nations.

Photos

Mahu Sasa from the island of Nanumea was totally blind for 3 years of her life. In 2017, Dr. John Willoughby restored the sight in her right eye. In 2018, he restored the sight in her left eye, with a remarkable outcome that she could read the bottom line on the Snellen visual acuity chart. Mahu is looking forward to being able to weave agiain and being able to actually see her grandchildren when they visit.
Mahu Sasa
Isileli Moala from Tonga was blind in both of his eyes for close to ten years. In 2011 a visiting eye team failed in an attempt to restore the vision to his right eye. He remained unable to see anything for 7 more years. In 2018, Dr. John Willougby successfully restored the vision to his left eye, with euphoric results. The first thing he asked to do was to read a bible so he could thank God for the eye-team who restored his vision.
Isileli Moala
